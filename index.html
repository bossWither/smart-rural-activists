About the project

What is SMART Rural Activists?

SMART Rural Activists is an Erasmus+ youth exchange bringing together young people from five European countries to explore entrepreneurship, rural community development, and active citizenship.

Hosted by MALIK ETS in the small Sardinian village of Samugheo, the eight-day exchange combines workshops, field visits to local enterprises, cultural evenings, and World Café discussions — all focused on how young people can be "smart activists" in their rural communities.

Participants leave with a Youthpass Certificate that officially recognises the skills and competences they developed throughout the project.

Youthpass

What you gain

The Erasmus+ Youthpass is an official EU certificate that recognises eight key competences developed during the exchange. You create it — by reflecting on your own learning.

🧠
Personal & Learning to Learn
Understanding yourself better, managing emotions, and learning how to learn more effectively.
💬
Literacy
Expressing ideas clearly in speaking and writing, often in English as a common language.
🌍
Citizenship
Being an active and responsible citizen, understanding different societal perspectives.
💻
Digital
Using digital tools to communicate, collaborate, and create content safely and smartly.
🚀
Entrepreneurship
Developing creativity, teamwork, and the ability to turn ideas into real action.
🎭
Cultural Awareness
Exploring cultures and expressing yourself through music, art, traditions, and creativity.
🔢
STEM
Strengthening problem-solving and logical thinking, understanding the world practically.
🗣️
Multilingual
Communicating in different languages and feeling confident in an international group.
📜

Youthpass is not automatic — you create it!

During the project, you will reflect on what you learned, discuss with your group leader, and describe your learning in your own words. This experience is about what you discover about yourself.

Practical Information

Before you go

Everything you need to know for a smooth trip to Sardinia.

📄

Documents to Bring

  • Valid ID card or passport
  • Travel tickets & boarding passes (paper + digital)
  • European Health Insurance Card (EHIC)
  • Private travel insurance (IATI recommended)
👕

What to Pack

  • Light summer clothes (21–23°C days)
  • Warm layer for evenings (11–13°C)
  • Comfortable closed walking shoes
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses, hat
  • Mosquito repellent, reusable water bottle
  • Power bank & small backpack
✈️

Getting to Samugheo

  • Fly to Cagliari Elmas Airport
  • Train: Airport → Cagliari centre (~6 min)
  • Meet at Piazza Yenne at 17:40 on 11 May
  • Private bus: Cagliari → Samugheo (~1.5–2 hrs)
🚌

Transport in Cagliari

  • Buy bus tickets at Tabaccheria (paper)
  • Or use the BUSFINDER CTM app (recommended)
  • Luggage storage near station: luggagestoragecagliari.com
  • Hotel Due Colonne also stores luggage (~€5/day)
📱

Connectivity

  • Italian SIM for tourists: WINDTRE Tourist Pass
  • Bring your ID to purchase a SIM
  • WINDTRE store ~9 min walk from Cagliari station
  • Cost: up to €30 for a tourist SIM
  • WhatsApp group: "Participants SMART" — join link on blog
🌍

Multicultural Evening

  • Bring traditional Bulgarian food or drink
  • Bring the Bulgarian national flag
  • Prepare music, dances, or games
  • Interesting facts about Bulgaria
  • Food must be ready to eat (no kitchen use)

Personal Stories

Our Experience

The Bulgarian team's journey, challenges, and growth during the SMART Rural Activists exchange.

A Journey of Discovery

Participating in the SMART Rural Activists project was an eye-opening experience. We engaged in deep discussions about entrepreneurship and learned how we can actively contribute to the development of our rural communities. The field visits allowed us to see real-world applications of the concepts we discussed.

Cultural Exchange

Connecting with peers from Italy, Portugal, Poland, and Croatia enriched our perspective. The cultural evenings were a highlight, sharing traditional Bulgarian food and learning about the rich traditions of the other participating nations. These interactions broke down barriers and fostered lasting friendships.

Personal Growth

Beyond the technical knowledge, this exchange pushed us out of our comfort zones. We improved our English communication skills, learned to collaborate in diverse teams, and gained confidence in expressing our ideas. Earning the Youthpass was a proud moment that validated our personal and professional development.

Code of Conduct

Rules & Responsibilities

All participants are expected to uphold EU values, respect each other, and take responsibility for their behaviour throughout the project.

⚠️ Important From the second rule violation: adults are sent home immediately with no reimbursement. Minors' guardians will be contacted. Full attendance at all sessions is mandatory for travel reimbursement.
🤝Respect and uphold fundamental EU values: dignity, freedom, democracy, equality, and human rights.
⏰Be on time for all activities. Full attendance is required — exceptions must be agreed with coordinators in advance.
🏠Keep rooms and common spaces clean and in the same condition as when delivered. Do not switch rooms.
🚭No smoking indoors. Alcohol allowed in moderation for adults only in authorised areas outside activity hours. No drugs.
🌙Maintain quiet between 23:00 and 07:00. Minors must return before midnight when accompanied by a youth leader.
🍽️Meals are provided (breakfast, lunch, dinner). No food in rooms. Buffet style — take in moderation first.
🧾Keep all travel tickets, invoices, and boarding passes — required for travel reimbursement after the project.
📢Share at least one dissemination activity (social media post, photo, article) following the partnership agreement.
